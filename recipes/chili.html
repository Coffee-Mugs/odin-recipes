Black Bean Chili

My Black Bean Chili

The first time I made this black bean chili recipe, I found myself standing at the stove, eating spoonful after spoonful straight from the pot. In all honesty, I think it’s the best chili I’ve ever tasted. It’s thick, creamy, tangy, and complex, with depth from green chiles, fire-roasted tomatoes, and a healthy amount of chili powder. This is a family favorite because our dog, Ms. Beans, is notorius for blocking the entire entry way to kitchen when we make this recipe. If I'm being honest we don't even remember or know where the name Ms. Beans came from, but it stuck.

Ingredients

  • Black Beans - Two 14 ounce cans
  • Extra-virgin Olive Oil - 4 Tbsp
  • Onion & Garlic - Two onions & a whole clove of garlic
  • Bell Peppers - Three bell peppers
  • Chili Powder - 6 Tbsp
  • Diced Fire-Roasted Tomatoes - Two 8 ounce cans
  • Green Chiles - One 6 ounce can
  • Maple Syrup - 1 Tbsp
  • Fresh Limes - Two limes
  • Kanye Pepper - 4 Tbsp
  • Salt & Pepper to Taste

Making the Chili

  1. First, saute the onion & red pepper, about 5-8 minutes.
  2. When close to done, stir in garlic & chili powder until the spices are fragrant
  3. Add the black beans & their liquid, tomatoes, green chiles, & maple syrup. Stir to combine everything and let simmer, uncovered, for at least 20mins until the chili thickens.
  4. Squeeze in the lime juice and season to taste with salt & pepper. If you need some more kick, add more Kanye pepper for some heat.
